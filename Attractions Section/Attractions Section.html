Must-See Landmarks in Seville

Experience the magic of Southern Spain's most enchanting city

Alcázar of Seville – A Real-Life Fairytale Palace

This breathtaking royal palace is one of the most beautiful in Spain. Originally built by the Moors, it later became home to Spanish kings.

Inside, you'll find intricate tilework, elegant courtyards, and lush gardens that make you feel like you've stepped into a fairytale. Fans of Game of Thrones might recognize it as the setting for the Kingdom of Dorne.

Visiting hours: 9:30AM - 5:00PM Ticket price: €12.50 Learn More

Seville Cathedral & La Giralda – A Tower With a View

The Seville Cathedral is the largest Gothic church in the world and is famous for its stunning architecture and grand altars. Inside, you can also visit the tomb of Christopher Columbus.

The cathedral's bell tower, La Giralda, was once a minaret and offers amazing views of the city—just be ready for a long walk up!

Visiting hours: 11:00AM - 3:30PM Ticket price: €10 Learn More

Plaza de España – The Most Beautiful Square in Seville

This massive semi-circular plaza is one of Seville's most famous spots. Built for a world fair in 1929, it is surrounded by elegant buildings, colorful ceramic tiles, and small bridges over a canal.

You can rent a boat and row through the water or simply admire the stunning design.

Visiting hours: Open 24 hours Ticket price: Free Learn More

Triana District – The Heart of Flamenco

Triana is one of Seville's most lively neighborhoods, famous for flamenco music, handmade ceramics, and delicious tapas.

Walk along the river, explore its charming streets, or watch a live flamenco show in one of its traditional bars.

Best time to visit: Evening Known for: Flamenco, ceramics Learn More

Torre del Oro – The Golden Watchtower

This 13th-century tower was once part of the city's defenses and is located right next to the Guadalquivir River.

It's called the "Tower of Gold" because it used to reflect golden light on the water. Today, it houses a small naval museum, and you can climb to the top for a great view.

Visiting hours: 10:00AM - 6:00PM Ticket price: €3 Learn More

Metropol Parasol – The Giant Wooden Mushrooms

Known as Las Setas ("The Mushrooms"), this modern wooden structure is the largest of its kind in the world.

It stands in the middle of Seville's old town and has a rooftop walkway with fantastic panoramic views. It's a great place to see the sunset over the city.

Visiting hours: 9:30AM - 11:00PM Ticket price: €5 for rooftop access Learn More

Plaza de Toros de la Maestranza – A Historic Bullring

One of Spain's oldest and most famous bullrings, La Maestranza is an impressive building with a fascinating museum about the history of bullfighting.

Even if you're not interested in bullfighting itself, the architecture and cultural significance make it worth a visit.

Visiting hours: 9:30AM - 7:00PM Learn More

Casa de Pilatos – A Hidden Architectural Gem

This 16th-century palace is not as famous as the Alcázar but is just as stunning.

It combines Moorish, Gothic, and Renaissance styles, with beautiful courtyards and tile decorations. It's like stepping into another era.

Visiting hours: 9:00AM - 6:00PM Ticket price: €10 Learn More

Museum of Fine Arts – A Treasure of Spanish Art

If you love art, this museum is a must-visit. It's one of Spain's most important galleries, featuring paintings from the Spanish Golden Age.

The collection includes works by Murillo, Velázquez, and Zurbarán. The building itself, a former convent, is also beautiful.

Visiting hours: 9:00AM - 5:00PM (Closed Mondays) Ticket price: €1.50 (Free for EU citizens) Learn More
